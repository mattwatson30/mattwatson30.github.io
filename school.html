Embedded Control System for Cardiac Imaging

Quinn Laboratory @ Dalhousie University

Problem: The Cardiac Autoregulation & Arrhythmias Laboratory at Dalhousie University required a precise, user-configurable system to synchronize high-intensity LEDs with high-speed cameras for cardiac fluorescence imaging. Existing solutions were rigid and lacked the millisecond-level synchronization needed to capture rapid cardiac electrical activity without artifacts.

My Solution: I developed a full-stack control system. On the desktop side, I built a Python GUI (PyQt6) that allows researchers to define complex pulse patterns. This communicates via serial to an ATmega328P microcontroller, where I wrote bare-metal firmware using Hardware Timers and Interrupts to generate precise 20kHz PWM signals. I also designed the power stage using IRLZ44N MOSFETs to drive the 24V LED arrays.

Results: The system achieved a trigger latency of < 200µs and frequency accuracy error of less than 5%. It is now the standard operating equipment for the lab, allowing researchers to capture high-fidelity cardiac fluorescence imaging data.

Quantitative Risk Management App

Director of Technology @ Dalhousie Investment Society

Problem: The Investment Society managed four distinct portfolios (Commodities, Global Macro, Real-Estate, Forex) but lacked visibility into cross-portfolio risk. Without a centralized way to track daily Profit and Loss (PNL), the teams could not effectively hedge trades against market volatility.

My Solution: I engineered a full-stack quantitative analysis application using Python. I utilized the Bloomberg API to pull raw trade ticket data and used Pandas/NumPy to calculate daily PNL across all four sectors. I designed a Tkinter GUI that provides analysts with automated hedging suggestions based on real-time stock correlation matrices and Monte Carlo risk simulations.

Results: The application became the central source of truth for the society's performance tracking. By enabling data-driven hedging strategies between portfolios, the Commodities team saw an 8% increase in monthly P&L immediately following adoption.
